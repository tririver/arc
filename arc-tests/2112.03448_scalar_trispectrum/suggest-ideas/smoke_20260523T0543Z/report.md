### idea_001 Round 1: Cut-Dispersion Reconstruction of the Boostless Massive-Scalar Exchange Trispectrum

- Idea: Use the 2112.03448 cutting rule as discontinuity data, then reconstruct the full scalar four-point exchange correlator by momentum-space dispersion, fixing the remaining contact ambiguity with the boostless contact-trispectrum basis.
- Motivation: The seed paper arXiv:2112.03448 gives an efficient way to isolate local versus nonlocal cosmological-collider signals from massive-particle evolution, but mainly as signal extraction. A natural next step is to turn those cuts into a full-shape calculation of the scalar trispectrum without doing the original nested in-in time integrals. This sits between known contact reconstruction for scalar trispectra and newer discontinuity/dispersion methods, and it targets the open exchange-trispectrum problem rather than another contact-term classification.
- Calculation target: Compute the tree-level four-point function <zeta zeta zeta zeta> from one exchanged massive scalar sigma in a boost-breaking EFT of inflation, starting with the minimal cubic pair dot-zeta^2 sigma at both vertices, then adding the first spatial-derivative vertex (partial_i zeta)^2 sigma as the first nontrivial boostless extension. The first explicit deliverable is the collapsed-channel nonanalytic part plus a dispersion-reconstructed full s-channel shape, up to a finite contact polynomial fixed by the known contact basis and soft/flat-space limits.
- Method sketch: Start with conformal time mode functions for massless external scalars and a principal-series massive sigma. Use the 2112.03448 cut rule to write the s-channel discontinuity/nonlocal signal as a product of two cubic sub-correlators. Treat this discontinuity as input to a one-variable dispersion relation in the internal energy or collapsed momentum variable. Reconstruct the exchange correlator, then add the most general boostless local contact trispectrum at the same derivative order and fix its coefficients by requiring correct total-energy singularity, absence of spurious folded poles, permutation symmetry across s/t/u channels, and agreement with the heavy-mass local EFT expansion.
